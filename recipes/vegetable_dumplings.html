Vegetable Dumplings Recipe

Description

Vegetable dumplings are a delightful option for those who prefer a meat-free filling. These dumplings are packed with a variety of fresh vegetables and are typically seasoned with soy sauce and spices. They can be enjoyed steamed, boiled, or pan-fried, and make a great appetizer or main dish.

The filling for vegetable dumplings often includes ingredients like cabbage, carrots, and mushrooms, offering a healthy and flavorful alternative to meat-based dumplings.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrot
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 scallions (or green onions), finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the dough:

  • 200 g (about 1 1/2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup hot water
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

Prepare the Filling:

  1. Mix the filling: In a large bowl, combine the cabbage, carrot, mushrooms, garlic, scallions, soy sauce, sesame oil, and ginger. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Prepare the Dough:

  1. Make the dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour with the salt. Gradually add the hot water, stirring continuously, until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour.
  2. Divide the dough: Divide the dough into small balls, roughly the size of a walnut. Roll each ball into a thin circle, about 8-10 cm (3-4 inches) in diameter.

Assemble the Dumplings:

  1. Fill the dumplings: Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough in half over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges to seal the dumpling well, and pleat the edges if desired to ensure they are tightly closed.

Cook the Dumplings:

  1. Steaming: If you have a steamer, place the dumplings in the steamer (in a single layer) and steam for about 15 minutes.
  2. Pan-frying: Heat a little oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Place the dumplings in the skillet and cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden and crispy. Add a little water (about 1/4 cup), cover with a lid, and steam for another 5-7 minutes until the filling is fully cooked.
